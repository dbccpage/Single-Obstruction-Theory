# Changelog: v028 to v029
**Paper:** 002 - Coordinate-Wise Additivity and the $\ell^1$ Norm on Finite Graph Cochains
**Date:** March 2026

## Overview of Changes
The transition from v028 to v029 seals the core geometric stability of the combinatorial foundation model. We appended an explicit clarification constructing symmetric automorphism graph embeddings formally defending the reduction of uniform weights across arbitrary discrete graphs.

## Specific Modifications

### 1. Symmetric Completion Definition Made Explicit
- **Target:** Section 2, Proof of Theorem 2.1 (Graph invariance -> Uniform weights section)
- **Change:** Appended the sentence: *"Such symmetric completions can always be constructed by embedding edges into disjoint unions of isomorphic components (e.g., cycles or complete graphs) admitting automorphisms that exchange the embedded edges."*
- **Rationale:** The original proof assumed the constructive availability of symmetric global isomorphic mappings to drive the edge weight limits independently across any generic graphs into equality. By directly confirming the structural protocol supporting these constructions via disjoint union embeddings, the argument transitions from "it's obvious we can do this" to formally bounded geometric assurance. No gap remains.
