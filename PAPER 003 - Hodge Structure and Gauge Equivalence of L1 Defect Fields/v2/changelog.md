# Changelog: v017 to v018
**Paper:** 003 - Hodge Structure and Gauge Equivalence of $\ell^1$ Defect Fields
**Date:** March 2026

## Overview of Changes
The transition from v017 to v018 finalizes the mathematical posture of the cohomological structure. We adjusted semantic boundaries to strictly define the $\ell^1$-native decomposition as an alternative, geometry-specific standard rather than an invalidation of classical $\ell^2$ Hodge theory.

## Specific Modifications

### 1. Re-Scoped "Hodge Replacement" Claim
- **Target:** Section 12.1 (What This Paper Establishes)
- **Change:** Softened the absolute claim that $\ell^1$ structure "completely replaces classical Hodge theory" to state that it "provides the canonical decomposition compatible with the $\ell^1$ geometry, in contrast to the $\ell^2$ Hodge decomposition."
- **Rationale:** Prevents the framework from overreaching. $\ell^2$ Hodge theory remains valid and structurally active within its own geometric paradigm; the $\ell^1$ decomposition is strictly the naturally induced structure when conforming to the forced boundaries inherited from preceding foundational papers.

### 2. Standardized Inheritance Constraint
- **Target:** Section 1 (Introduction)
- **Change:** Modified language asserting that "$\ell^1$ is uniquely forced" to read "given the $\ell^1$ structure established in [14--16]."
- **Rationale:** Prevents Paper 003 from redundantly re-claiming the geometric derivations of Papers 000-002. This locks the paper clearly into its role as interpreting an already established structure.

### 3. Clarified Uniqueness Limits on $\Phi$
- **Target:** Remark 7.7 (Uniqueness of $\Phi$)
- **Change:** Adjusted phrasing to clarify that $\Phi$ is unique among norms "obtained via the standard quotient construction" rather than "uniquely induced."
- **Rationale:** Acknowledges the existence of other gauge-invariant norms while tightening the bounds of the structural necessity to the specific quotient construction mechanism.
