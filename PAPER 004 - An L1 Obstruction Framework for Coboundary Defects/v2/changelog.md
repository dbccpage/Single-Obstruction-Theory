# Changelog: v004 to v005
**Paper:** An $\ell^1$ Obstruction Framework for Coboundary Defects
**Date:** March 2026

## Overview of Changes
The transition from v004 to v005 brings strict claim-discipline to the unified framework paper. We removed isolated topological derivation overreaches, particularly concerning gauge structure constraints and dynamical proofs of descent, pulling the paper fully back into demonstrable structural boundaries.

## Specific Modifications

### 1. Gauge Derivation Bounds Softened
- **Target:** Theorem 4.10 (Unitary Gauge Structure)
- **Change:** Removed the assertion that the structure "uniquely forces the fiber symmetry group to be $U(n)$." Replaced with: "selects unitary conjugation as the admissible class of local automorphisms, corresponding to a $U(n)$ gauge structure."
- **Rationale:** Clarifies the exact difference between classifying an automorphism group under imposed constraints within the math model vs physically deriving an intrinsic standard model $U(n)$ gauge field. The result is mathematically identical, but epistemologically secure.

### 2. Autopoietic Iterator Convergence Clarified
- **Target:** Interpretation of Autopoietic Gauge Iterator (Conjecture 4.3 limits)
- **Change:** Softened the convergence language from "converging toward irreducible cohomological structure" to stating it "is designed to act as a locally driven global optimizer, with fixed points characterized by $\ell^1$ minimality; convergence remains an open problem."
- **Rationale:** Separates the rigorous proof of the fixed-point characterization (Layer B) from the unproven conjecture that the sequence monotonically descends toward the fixed points (Layer C). The framework is now shielded against attacks on unproven dynamical descent.

### 3. Lebesuge/Measure Formalization
- **Target:** Theorem 4.6 (Cycle Interaction Principle)
- **Change:** Substituted the phrase "Lebesgue-almost all overlap configurations" with "generically (i.e., outside a measure-zero set in the space of defect assignments)."
- **Rationale:** Corrects a category mismatch. In a purely discrete space, establishing Lebesgue measure lacks a formal ambient space without additional metric structure. Standardizing to generic parameter space configurations prevents formal topological challenges while preserving the intended geometrical assertion.
