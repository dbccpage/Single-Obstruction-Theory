# Changelog: v028 to v029
**Paper:** 000 - Projection Obstruction Theory: Retraction Nonlinearity, $\ell^1$ Rigidity, and Density Scaling
**Date:** March 2026

## Overview of Changes
The transition from v028 to v029 incorporates critical geometric scoping corrections specifically addressing the analytic independence of the foundational topological math from downstream conjectural physics interpretations. These edits solidify the mathematical legitimacy of the framework's baseline theorems.

## Specific Modifications

### 1. Theorem 3.1: Flat Connection Assumption Re-scoped
- **Target:** Section 3.1 (Projection Non-Dilability)
- **Change:** Appended an explicit clarification to the theorem boundaries: *"All arguments are local in affine coordinate charts induced by the ambient Banach space; global flatness of $M$ is not required."*
- **Rationale:** The original theorem implicitly relied on the existence of a globally flat connection on $M$ (which is a nonlinear retraction submanifold). By strictly locating the arguments to operate strictly locally within induced affine coordinate charts, we cleanly removed the vulnerability surrounding global assumptions without weakening the theorem's core conclusion about nonlinear obstruction.

### 2. Appendix A: Derivation Pathway Buffered
- **Target:** Section A.2 (The Formal Chain)
- **Change:** The statement linking observer structure to quantum limits (via the $\ell^1 \to \mathcal{H}$ pathway) was softened to state: *"This chain represents **one consistent realization** of the axioms mapping toward emergent physical structures; **alternative realizations may exist**."*
- **Rationale:** Previously, the framework inadvertently implied that the derivation chain leading to human-recognized physical domains was an absolute, strictly forced inevitability of the axioms. This change successfully partitions the rigorous core math (Layer 1) from the physics interpretation platform (Layer 2), establishing that the axioms don't inherently dictate physical bounds; they merely provide the strict logic allowing them.
